Sepsis Recognition & Management – Interactive Teaching Simulation

An interactive clinical teaching simulation designed to help pre-registration nursing students recognise, escalate, and manage suspected sepsis using a structured patient scenario.

Overview

This project presents an interactive browser-based simulation following Joan Evans, a 68-year-old patient admitted with cellulitis who deteriorates into suspected sepsis. Learners move through a staged clinical scenario, interpret observations, identify red flags, apply the Sepsis Six, practise SBAR escalation, and complete structured reflection.

The simulation is designed around NICE NG51: Sepsis: recognition, diagnosis and early management.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the simulation, learners should be able to:

Define sepsis using NICE NG51 terminology.
Interpret clinical observations and calculate risk using NEWS2.
Identify red flags associated with suspected sepsis.
Apply the Sepsis Six within the recommended timeframe.
Escalate clinical concerns using SBAR communication.
Reflect on clinical decision-making using Gibbs’ reflective cycle.
Scenario Summary

The simulation follows:

Patient: Joan Evans
Age: 68
Admission reason: Cellulitis of the left lower leg
Relevant history: Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, peripheral vascular disease
Allergy: Penicillin, documented rash

During the morning handover, Joan is found to be confused, difficult to rouse, hypotensive, febrile, tachycardic, and oliguric. Her NEWS2 score is presented as high risk, prompting urgent recognition and escalation of suspected sepsis.

Suggested Use in Teaching

This simulation can be used for:

Pre-registration nursing education
Clinical skills sessions
Sepsis recognition teaching
Acute deterioration teaching
SBAR communication practice
Simulation-based group discussion
Reflection and debrief activities

Suggested delivery format:

Introduce the learning outcomes.
Ask students to work through the scenario individually or in small groups.
Pause at decision points for discussion.
Use the Sepsis Six stage as a timed activity.
Practise SBAR escalation verbally.
Complete a structured debrief using Gibbs’ reflective cycle.
Educational Disclaimer

This simulation is for educational and training purposes only. It does not replace local clinical guidelines, professional judgement, senior clinical review, or organisational sepsis protocols.

Users should always follow current local policy, national guidance, and professional standards when managing suspected sepsis in clinical practice.
